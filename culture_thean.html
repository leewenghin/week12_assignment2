Thean Hou Temple

Thean Hou Temple, Kuala Lumpur

About

Thean Hou Temple is one of the largest and oldest Buddhist temples in Southeast Asia. Located along Jalan Klang Lama on the southwest side of Kuala Lumpur city. It is managed by the Hainanese community who descended from sea-faring fisherfolk with roots in Hainan Province of China. Perched on a hill, it offers stunning panoramic views of the city.

This Buddhist temple is dedicated to Thean Hou, a patron goddess of the Hainanese community believed to watch over fishermen. Many locals come here to worship Guan Yin, the Goddess of Mercy. Constructed between 1981 and 1987, the temple's design structure combines traditional and contemporary styles with intricate carvings and murals.

Attractions

This temple with elements of Buddhism, Confucianism and Taoism is a grandiose structure and represents a successful combination of modern architectural techniques and authentic traditional design The bright red pillars of the temple's entrance symbolise prosperity and good fortune, while the dragon-inspired columns and beautiful pagoda rooftops contribute to the temple's architectural allure.

Red lanterns hang from the edges of the rooftops and stretch across the temple grounds, making Thean Hou a popular landmark not just for tourists, but for pre-wedding photography as well.
